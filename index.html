Rare Earth Magnets

Rare earth magnets are strong permanent magnets made from alloys of rare earth elements. Developed in the 1970s and 1980s, these are the strongest type of permanent magnets made, producing significantly stronger magnetic fields than other types such as ferrite or alnico magnets. Rare earth magnets are usually plated or coated to protect them from breaking, chipping, or crumbling into powder.

Ferrite Magnets

Ferrite magnets are also known as ceramic magnets because of their low production cost. They are the most popular for everyday applications. Ferrite magnets are extremely popular due to their characteristics and are corrosion free. For long term performance they are superb; if looked after they are capable of exceeding most products lifecycles. Ferrite magnets can be used up to +250 degrees C (and in some cases up to +300 deg C)

Badge Magnets

Easy to use badge magnets made with neodynium magnets for affixing name tags and business cards at conferences, meetings, tradeshows and events. Magnetic badges are a great alternative to traditional pin badges, they are high in magnetic strength, durable, lightweight, and they will not damage or tear clothing. Badge magnets can pose a risk to humans when you have a pace maker helping your heart and if you wear that badge on your chest over the heart.

Fishing Magnetics

These magnets (made of rare earth) are specially designed for MAGNET FISHING. This specific magnet is strong enough to retrieve ferro-magnetic objects like jewellery, weapons, etc. from water. Magnet fishing involves dropping a strong magnet attached to a rope, into a river, creek, lake, or other fishing areas to find and recover objects. These objects attach themselves to the magnet. The larger the magnet, the stronger the magnetic pull off will be in retrieving bigger objects. Other uses are holding down firearms or magazines, hanging pot plants, closing of front gates or safe doors.

Neogrippers

These threaded magnetic assemblies are made with most powerful magnet material available. Each assembly features a neodynium magnet affixed to a flat steel disc and coated with a black protective rubber to prevent surface scratches. It is widely used for mounting cameras, car mounts, tracking devices, holding up revolvers and magazines.

Educational Magnets

Magnets have been popular in science experiments for many years, they are perfect for the classroom, both for teaching students about magnetism and as general teaching aids. They are most useful in model making for younger students through to the enquiring minds of senior school and university studies. By using these magnets with iron filings it clearly demonstrates the lines of magnetic fields.

Magnetic Safety

  • Swallowing: Permanent magnets should not be considered as toys. Some magnets are small enough to be ingested by children. If ingested seek immediate medical attention as it can cause serious or fatal results.
  • Handling: Large neodynium magnets are very powerful and improper use of them can cause serious injuries such as hands, fingers between magnets, bone fractures. It is important to use the correct protective gear such as goggles, gloves and safety boots. Use a sliding action whenever separating neodynium magnets. Itâ€™s advisable to not machine any rare earth magnets as they are very brittle and will crack, break or shatter and create a spark. Do not exceed the maximum working as they will lose their magnetic properties.
  • Pace Makers: The pacemaker and defibrillator if exposured to strong magnetic fields >10 gauss (1 mTesla) it may alter the implanted device.
  • Corrosion: Neodynium magnets will corrode if not coated properly. These magnets are not waterproof and should not be exposed to moisture or water for long periods of time. Rare earth magnets react to moisture by rusting. When a magnet is corroded by rust it loses a large portion of its powers of attraction.
